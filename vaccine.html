Vaccines

The vaccine

There are many different types of vaccines for COVID-19, released by various governments and pharmaceutical companies, and split among multiple delivery methods. Some vaccines are mRNA based, and as such are a new type of vaccine that encourages peoples' cells to produce more immune cells that specifically attack the COVID virus. Others are the more common carrier vaccines, where patients are injected with a harmless variation of COVID to prepare the body to fight off the more lethal version later. These different vaccines all have their advantages and disadvantages, and may need one or several applications and booster shots to be fully effective.

The following vaccines are some the vaccines that have been released:

  • Johnson & Johnson
  • Pfizer
  • Moderna
  • Novavax
  • AstraZeneca
  • Sinopharm
  • Sputnik V

Who can get the vaccine?

The following people can get vaccinated for COVID:

  • Everyone 5 years of age and older is now eligible to get a COVID-19 vaccination.
  • Everyone ages 5 and older is eligible for a booster shot.

Where to get vaccinated

COVID vaccines are widely available at pharmacies, local health departments, clinics, Federally Qualified Health Centers and other locations across New York state.
Walk-in appointments are also accepted at New York State mass vaccination sites for all eligible adults. These mass vaccination sites are determined by the government, and are typically public areas already used for gatherings, such as town halls.
